### Pace Is a Constraint, Not a Goal

Most _endurance training_ happens slower than ego would prefer. _Easy pace_ feels almost wrong at first. You question whether it's "doing anything." But that restraint is the point. Running too fast too often gives immediate feedback and long-term penalties. Running easy builds capacity quietly.

This maps directly to **systems design**. Operating close to theoretical limits might look efficient on paper, but it leaves no room for variance: **traffic spikes**, **GC pauses**, **human error**, **bad days**. _Endurance_ â€” in running or software â€” comes from **respecting margins**.
